Dear Reader!
<br/>
I would like to share with you my thoughts about a project I've recently decided to start working on, to become a Cloud Engineer. As someone who has spent a few years working as a System Administrator, I've started to feel that I want to do more with my life. The current position I'm at won't provide me more than the mundane administrative tasks left for me to do including some IT-related work but writing e-mails most of the time. I’ve decided it’s time to leverage my experience, gain more skills, learn cutting-edge technologies and take the next step in my career.
<br/>
About two years ago I decided that I would like to become a network engineer so after finishing my certified systems administrator diploma I thought I should take the CCNA 200-301 exam, try to find a job and progress towards acquiring the CCNP certificate and maybe the CCIE later on. I bought the official cert guide books from Wendell Odom, and also the practive exam from Boson and started studying.

> I had to graduate at the age of 30.

Later that year I was admitted to the University to a Computer Science degree program. One of the admission requirements was to have a high school diploma exam from mathematics, which I didn't have at the time so that was another challenge. I also graduated from computer science along with mathematics.
It was a rather unusual experience to sit into the classroom again in my 30's and take the exams but it was also a fulfilling yourney and I learned valuable knowledge which became useful in calculus courses in the university.
<br/>
After graduating I got a job offer from a battery factory and decided to relocate another city. The position was offering a higher salary and opportunity to gain more experience. The main disatvange was the shift schedule. I had to work in rotational twelve shifts with two weeks during the day and two subsequent days during the night. I learned alot but also felt overhelmed by the ever changing schedule and at some point I had to admit to myself, that I won't be able to susatin a healthy lifestyle with this kind of schedule. At around december I got an offer from a biofuel factory for a sysadmin role, where I'm currently working at.
<br/>
During the constant change of learning on the job, changing positions and relocating I've pretty much stopped studying for the CCNA which only some labbing and reading something here and there along the way.
After finishing my exams this semester, I thought now would be the best time to go full in with the studying and I wanted to pass the exam till the end of the year. I found a very comprehensive video series on Youtube (shout out to <a href="https://www.youtube.com/@JeremysITLab">Jeremy's IT Lab</a>) and i worked through all the 70 something videos. Also re-read the Odom books and was studying like a madman. At the time I've finished the course, done all the labs, finished learning more than 2000 flashcards, I started to feel more and more exhausted and my performance started to decline. I kept forgetting concepts, had to frequently re-learn things and was finding hard to remember things.
At this time my son was born and which brought new changes to my life again, mainly lack of sleep during the nights.

> I didn't realise, but I was actually overstudying and on the verge of a mental breakdown.

I was still in my grind mode and wanted to pass the exam very badly but also felt that given the physical and mental condition I got into, maybe this isn't the best time for this. I also felt the urging deadline of the start of the new semester and was starting to feel depressed.
Once I was burning the midnight oil on a weekday evening I suddenly felt that I will collapse shortly and I immidately stopped studying and wen't to sleep. During the next couple of days I've thoght the currect situation throught and came to a conclusion that I will postpone the deadline of my exam. Luckily I haven't bought the exam voucher yet.
<br/>
Looking back to this a couple of weeks after, I was clearly in a deadly spiral pawed with imposter syndrome, self-doupt, constant drive to grind and the fear of getting old without accomplished anything in life. I was too overhelamed to see the situation clearly.

At the end I've decided to breath out a little. Started visiting the gym again, went out cycling which I pretty much enjoy and cut back on studying.

I'm still planning to keep my knowledge up to date by reviewing the notes I've taken for the CCNA and eventually passing the exam but it will probably beginning next year. I decided not to torture myself so much anymore and to focus more on my university studied in the upcoming months.

<br/>

## Realizing the potential in cloud computing

Along with the realization that I was overworking myself, I've came accross cloud computing which has mostly escaped my attention until now. I was experiencing pressure because I begun to see the declining opportunities in on-prem network engineering, the carreer I was struggling to pursue, as more companies are moving to the cloud as time move forward. I was also nervous about the possibility of needing to relocate again because the lack of opportunities in IT networking my current region of residence is offering. I felt selfish considering the possibility of moving with a little child just because of my career dreams.

Then I got the idea of looking into cloud jobs and found that my current knowldedge in IT networking won't be completely wasted by continuing on this path. Of course, networking is much simpler in the cloud and a level of knowledge beyound the CCNA would be an overkill and unnecessary but having the CCNA accompanied with some cloud associate certification could easly escalate me to my desired posotion.
<br/>
I'm currently at a phase of gaining and honing the core skills needed to transition to a cloud role and for that I'm following a detailed guide. I would like to shout out to <a href="https://www.youtube.com/@MadeByGPS">Gwyneth Peña-Siguenza</a> who made an excellent roadmap which I highly recommend to everyone considering a career change in this direction.

## Looking ahead

As I continue on this journey, I’m filled with excitement and anticipation. The world of cloud computing is vast and ever-evolving, offering endless opportunities for learning and growth. I’m looking forward to applying my skills in real-world scenarios and making a positive impact.

I’m hopeful that with hard work, perseverance, and the right resources, I’ll be able to accomplish my goals in the foreseeable future. It’s going to be a challenging journey, but as they say - nothing worth having comes easy!

So here’s to new beginnings, continuous learning, and the exciting journey ahead! Remember, every expert was once a beginner. So let’s embrace the process and enjoy the journey towards becoming a Cloud Engineer! 🚀☁️
